import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import matplotlib.patches as mpatches
import numpy as np

# --- Data ---
eras = [
    "Iraq War\n(2003–2011)",
    "Drone War\n(2010s)",
    "Anti-ISIS\n(2014–2019)",
    "Post-2020\nprecision",
    "AI-assisted\n(2025–2026)"
]

estimated    = np.array([180_000, 165_000, 178_000, 210_000, 220_000])
acknowledged = np.array([ 83_000,  57_000,  57_000,  70_000,  36_000])
gap          = estimated - acknowledged  # Accountability Gap Index

x = np.arange(len(eras))
width = 0.35

fig, ax1 = plt.subplots(figsize=(11, 6))
fig.patch.set_facecolor("#f9f9f7")
ax1.set_facecolor("#f9f9f7")

# --- Bars ---
bars1 = ax1.bar(x - width/2, estimated,    width, label="Estimated civilian deaths",       color="#E24B4A", alpha=0.85, zorder=2)
bars2 = ax1.bar(x + width/2, acknowledged, width, label="Officially acknowledged deaths",  color="#378ADD", alpha=0.88, zorder=2)

ax1.set_ylabel("Deaths", fontsize=11, color="#555")
ax1.set_xticks(x)
ax1.set_xticklabels(eras, fontsize=10)
ax1.yaxis.set_major_formatter(plt.FuncFormatter(lambda v, _: f"{int(v/1000)}k"))
ax1.tick_params(axis='y', labelcolor="#555")
ax1.grid(axis='y', color='#cccccc', linewidth=0.5, zorder=0)
ax1.spines[['top','right']].set_visible(False)

# --- Gap line (secondary axis) ---
ax2 = ax1.twinx()
ax2.plot(x, gap, color="#2C2C2A", linewidth=2.5, marker='o',
         markersize=7, label="Accountability gap index", zorder=3)

# Annotate gap values
for xi, g in zip(x, gap):
    ax2.annotate(f"{g/1000:.0f}k", xy=(xi, g), xytext=(0, 10),
                 textcoords="offset points", ha='center',
                 fontsize=9, color="#2C2C2A", fontweight='bold')

ax2.set_ylabel("Accountability Gap\n(Estimated – Acknowledged)", fontsize=11, color="#555")
ax2.yaxis.set_major_formatter(plt.FuncFormatter(lambda v, _: f"{int(v/1000)}k"))
ax2.tick_params(axis='y', labelcolor="#555")
ax2.spines[['top']].set_visible(False)

# --- Legend ---
patches = [
    mpatches.Patch(color="#E24B4A", alpha=0.85, label="Estimated civilian deaths"),
    mpatches.Patch(color="#378ADD", alpha=0.88, label="Officially acknowledged deaths"),
    plt.Line2D([0],[0], color="#2C2C2A", linewidth=2, marker='o', markersize=6,
               label="Accountability gap index")
]
ax1.legend(handles=patches, loc='upper left', fontsize=10, framealpha=0.7)

plt.title("Accountability Gap Over Time\nUS Warfare: Estimated vs. Acknowledged Civilian Deaths",
          fontsize=13, fontweight='500', pad=14)

plt.tight_layout()
plt.savefig("accountability_gap.png", dpi=150, bbox_inches='tight')
plt.show()

Iraq War (2003–2011)¶

Iraq Body Count → https://www.iraqbodycount.org — the primary documented civilian death database; recorded 162,000+ total violent deaths through 2011, ~80% civilian Casualties of the Iraq War (Wikipedia with sourcing) → https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casualties_of_the_Iraq_War Brown University Costs of War Project → https://costsofwar.watson.brown.edu/costs/human — estimates 432,000+ civilian deaths across post-9/11 wars PMC peer-reviewed study (Hagopian et al.) → https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3797136/ — estimated ~500,000 excess deaths 2003–2011 US Central Command gave a total of 76,939 Iraqi security and civilians killed — a figure widely criticized as far too low, and Iraq's Ministry of Health was reportedly ordered to stop counting civilian dead in late 2003.

Drone War (2010s)¶

Bureau of Investigative Journalism drone war archive → https://airwars.org/archives/bij-drone-war — tracked covert strikes 2010–2019 in Pakistan, Yemen, Somalia Civilian casualties from US drone strikes (Wikipedia) → https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilian_casualties_from_the_United_States_drone_strikes The DNI's own reports acknowledge that the government's reported civilian casualty numbers are far lower than NGO estimates, with one scholar noting official figures "systematically underestimated civilian casualties." (Wikipedia) In 2016, the US admitted killing between 64 and 116 civilians in Libya, Pakistan, Somalia and Yemen in counter-terrorism operations from 2009–2015, but provided no further details, dates, or specifics. Anti-ISIS Campaign (2014–2019)¶

Airwars — US-led Coalition Iraq & Syria → https://airwars.org/conflict/coalition-in-iraq-and-syria/ Washington Post / Airwars geolocated data investigation → https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2020/world/coalition-airstrikes-isis-civilian-death-toll/ Al Jazeera / Airwars data release → https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/11/18/us-military-releases-groundbreaking-data-on-civilian-harm-report The US military acknowledged roughly 1,400 civilian deaths from 2014 to 2019; Airwars estimates the actual total was somewhere between 8,310 and 13,187. (Al Jazeera) A New York Times ground survey found civilian casualties were 31 times more likely than the coalition was admitting.

Post-2020 / Somalia¶

The Intercept — Pentagon undercounts civilian casualties → https://theintercept.com/2021/06/03/pentagon-civilian-casualties-report/ Airwars — US Forces in Somalia → https://airwars.org/conflict/us-forces-in-somalia/ Airwars 2022 analysis → https://airwars.org/analysis-us-declares-its-military-actions-worldwide-killed-no-civilians-in-2022/ AFRICOM contends that hundreds of airstrikes and commando missions over more than a decade caused only two civilian casualties in Somalia total — while Airwars found the number of civilian deaths may be as much as 6,800% greater than the command asserts. The Intercept The US declared its military actions worldwide killed no civilians in 2022 — a claim Airwars directly disputed. (Airwars)
